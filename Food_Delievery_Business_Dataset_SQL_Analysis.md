***Business Problem* :**  A food delivery company wants to reduce delivery delays, improve customer



satisfaction , optimize delivery operations, understand customer behavior, improve operational efficiency.



***Dataset used*** : Food Delivery Dataset.



***Columns*** : Order\_ID , Order\_DateTime, City, Customer\_ID, Restaurant\_Name, Cuisine,Order\_Type,Payment\_Method,Order\_Value, Distance\_km, Weather,Traffic\_Level,Vehicle\_Type,Prepation\_Time\_min,Courier\_Experience\_Years, Delivery\_Time\_min, Customer\_Rating, Rating Category, Is\_Festival,Time\_of\_Day, Order\_Date,Order\_Time,shift\_period.



***Analysis***:

1\. Revenue \& Operational Performance Overview

The total revenue generated from the dataset is ₹15,409.47, with an average delivery time of 56.57 minutes, indicating moderate operational efficiency with scope for optimization in logistics performance.



2\. City-wise Delivery Performance Analysis

Pune exhibits the highest average delivery time (67.20 mins), followed by Hyderabad (66.67 mins), Bangalore (64.00 mins), and Mumbai (61.00 mins), indicating significant regional variability in last-mile delivery efficiency.

This suggests potential challenges related to traffic congestion, route complexity, and operational density in high-delay cities.



3\. Vehicle-Type Efficiency Analysis

Bike deliveries demonstrate the highest efficiency with an average delivery time of 44.20 mins, making them the most optimal mode for last-mile delivery.

Scooters (66.10 mins) and cars (52.38 mins) show comparatively higher delivery times, indicating lower operational agility and higher dependency on traffic conditions and fuel-based routing constraints.



4\. Weather Impact on Delivery Performance

Adverse weather conditions such as Fog, Storm, and Rain significantly increase delivery delays, highlighting a strong dependency between environmental conditions and operational efficiency.

Conversely, clear and cloudy weather conditions contribute to improved delivery turnaround times, indicating more stable logistics performance under favorable conditions.



5\. Traffic Congestion Impact Analysis

High and Jammed traffic zones exhibit extreme delay rates (up to 100%), while Medium traffic shows \~50% delays and Low traffic surprisingly shows \~55% delays.

This indicates that delivery performance is influenced not only by traffic intensity but also by secondary factors such as distance variability, order preparation time, and route optimization inefficiencies.



6\. City-Level Operational Risk Identification

Pune emerges as the highest-risk city in terms of delivery delays, primarily driven by combined effects of traffic congestion, weather variability, and operational bottlenecks in logistics execution.



7\. Seasonal Demand \& Delay Trends

Festival periods show a noticeable spike in delivery delays, indicating increased order load and operational strain on courier networks.

This highlights the need for demand forecasting and workforce scaling during peak seasonal demand periods.



8\. Customer Satisfaction Metrics

The average customer rating is 4.05, indicating generally positive customer sentiment and acceptable service quality.

However, variations in delivery time suggest that customer experience is strongly tied to delivery efficiency and consistency of service levels.



9\. Revenue Distribution by Geography

Delhi and Pune are the top revenue-generating cities, indicating strong market demand and high order density in these regions.

These cities represent high-value markets for expansion and operational investment focus.



10\. Time-Based Revenue Analysis

Evening hours generate the highest revenue contribution, driven by peak demand during dinner time.

Morning and afternoon periods show moderate order volumes, while night-time demand is significantly lower, indicating a clear time-based consumption pattern aligned with customer behavior trends.
